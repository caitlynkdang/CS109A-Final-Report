Project Problem Statement

With the introduction to music services such as Apple Music, Pandora, and Spotify, Music recommender systems (MRS) have exploded in popularity. A popular feature that many streaming services have is recommended new songs for users to listen to based on their listening history. Although music recommender systems are common, many systems differ in the way they recommend songs.

In particular, a problem that many recommender systems come across is the playlist generation problem-- Recommending new songs a user should add to the playlist given a set of songs already in the playlist. Within this problem, there lies a "cold start" problem, where the recommender must recommend songs to a playlist with very few songs in it.

For this project, we are focusing on Spotify's playlists. Spotify is known for its curated playlists--ones created by their team of music artists and ones created by their recommender system along with suggestions for users to add to their already exisiting playlist. As Spotify users ourselves, we are excited to dive into this problem and see how well we can recommend songs.

Project Goal

Automatic Playlist Generation — create a model for song discovery on the basis of the base playlist and user/context information that might be important to quality of a playlist. Some of user/context information might include intent, emotion, location, playlist “purpose” (driving/road trip, studying, etc). Use the developed model(s) for automatic playlist generation.
